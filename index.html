History of the Venice Project Center

Founded in 1988 by Fabio Carerra, the Venice Project Center gave WPI students an opportunity to apply their technical and non-technical skills to help the city of Venice.

The Pioneer Team Project

Fabio Carerra, a recent graduate of WPI, had a vision for his home city of Venice. Dr. Frederic Lane, author of Venice: A Maritime Republic, inspired Carerra to create the Venice Project Center. Carerra gathered 6 WPI students to determine oppurtunities that would be suitible for future IQP Projects. They created final proposals that were eventually used in future projects.

Fun Facts:

  • The project center ran out of Carerra's parents home
  • The National Council of Research (NRC) provided meeting facilities, fax machines, and other logistical items
